Inventory Integrity Audit

Reconciliation workflow from 20 years of floor experience — Problem, action, and measurable shift-level outcomes for WMS accuracy.

Reconciliation Audit Trails Variance Reports WMS Accuracy Root-Cause Tracking

Problem

On a high-volume floor, WMS mismatches from mislabels, short shipments, and receiving errors surface too late in the shift. By the time a coordinator sees the gap, pick errors and customer impact are already in motion.

Action

Compare

Match system inventory records against physical counts to surface SKUs where on-hand does not match what is on the shelf or in staging.

Log

Capture every inventory change automatically so audit trails stay intact across shift changes.

Report

Produce variance reports and hot lists by shift and zone so supervisors act during handoff.

Results

  • Faster shift close-out — less manual count-vs-system comparison time at end of shift
  • Repeat pattern detection — SKUs and zones driving recurring discrepancies flagged for corrective action
  • Fewer downstream errors — mismatches caught before they become pick failures or shipment exceptions
  • Supervisor-ready handoffs — reports formatted for corrective action during shift changeover

Why this matters for logistics coordination

This demonstrates how I translate warehouse operational data into actionable reports — the same skill set a Logistics Operations Coordinator needs when mislabels, shorts, and reconciliation errors are the daily reality on the floor.

Technical approach (summary)

Data matching System records joined to physical audit counts for variance detection
Integrity checks Constraints enforce valid data at entry — bad records caught before they propagate
Automated logging Changes tracked without manual intervention across shifts
Trend analysis Top mismatch SKUs ranked for targeted corrective action
